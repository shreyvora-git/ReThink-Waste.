The Problem

Unsegregated waste leads to pollution, overflowing landfills, and toxic hazards. Proper waste managemnent is critical to achiving SDG 11 : Sustainable Cities & Communities.






Why Waste Segregation Matters?

✔ Reduces Pollution

Minimizes land, air, and water contamination.

✔ Improves Recycling

Allows materials to be reused efficiently.

✔ Protects Health

Prevents harmful exposure to hazardous materials.






Types of waste!

Wet Waste

Food scraps, vegetable waste, biodegradable items.

Dry Waste

Paper, plastic, cardboard, glass, recyclable materials.

Hazardous Waste

Medical waste, chemicals, batteries, toxic.








SDG 11 : Sustainable Cities & Communities

SDG 11 aims to make cities inclusive, safe, resilent, and sustainable. Waste segregation plays a crucial role in improving public health, reducing pollution, and promoting smart environmental practices.
