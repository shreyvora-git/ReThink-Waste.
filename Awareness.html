Understanding the "why" behind every bin.

Did You Know?

One-Third of All Food

Is lost or wasted globally. Proper segregation (composting) can turn this waste into valuable fertilizer, reducing methane emissions from landfills.

91% of Plastic

Isn't recycled. Segregating plastic waste at the source is the *first and most critical* step to improving recycling rates and keeping plastic out of our oceans.

E-Waste Crisis

Electronic waste (like old phones) contains valuable materials like gold and silver, but also toxic ones like lead and mercury. It MUST be segregated and disposed of separately.

The Do's & Don'ts of Segregation

Do

  • ✔Rinse food containers (like plastic tubs or glass jars) before placing them in dry waste.
  • ✔Keep separate, clearly-labeled bins for Wet, Dry, and Hazardous waste.
  • ✔Wrap sanitary waste (diapers, pads) in paper and mark it as hazardous or sanitary.
  • ✔Check local guidelines for e-waste drop-off points.

Don't

  • ✖Mix wet food scraps with dry waste. This contaminates the entire batch, making it unrecyclable.
  • ✖Throw batteries, light bulbs, or expired medicines in your regular bins.
  • ✖Assume all plastic is recyclable. Items like thin bags or wrappers often are not.
  • ✖Put broken glass directly in a bag. Place it in a small cardboard box and label it "Broken Glass" to protect workers.

The Ripple Effect of Your Actions

Segregating your waste seems like a small act, but it triggers a massive chain reaction that benefits everyone. This goes far beyond just "helping the environment."

Economic Boost

When you segregate, you create clean streams of recyclable materials (paper, plastic, metal). This reduces the cost for industries to buy new raw materials and creates thousands of jobs in the recycling and waste management sectors.

Public Health Improvement

Unsegregated waste, especially mixed with food scraps, rots in landfills, attracting pests and creating diseases. By separating wet waste, you dramatically reduce landfill smell, contamination of groundwater, and the spread of illness.

Resource Conservation

Every item you recycle is one less item that needs to be created from scratch. Recycling paper saves trees, recycling plastic saves oil, and recycling metal saves finite ores. It's a direct way to conserve Earth's natural resources.
